Why We Built This

This tool was developed to provide Canadian Foreign Service officers with a clear, accessible starting point for understanding their FSD entitlements without having to navigate complex directives or rely solely on open-ended discussions with advisors. It acts as a preliminary decision-support layer that translates an individual's situation into a structured overview of applicable allowances, expected timing, and intended coverage. In contrast to the GAC's FSD Portal that displays what has already been paid, this tool establishes a baseline expectation of what should be received — enabling users to identify discrepancies, validate accuracy, and engage more effectively with FSD advisors through informed, targeted questions.

My name is Manoj and I am the spouse of a Foreign Service officer. This tool started as a small, personal project shaped by our own experience trying to navigate the complexities of Foreign Service Directives. We found it challenging to clearly understand entitlements and know whether everything was being applied as expected, which led me to build something simple to help make sense of it. It's very much a hobby project, but one that we've found genuinely useful, and we're happy to share it in the hope that it can make things a bit easier for others going through similar situations or provide a helpful starting point. Feel free to reach out through the feedback button with any suggestions to improve the tool.
